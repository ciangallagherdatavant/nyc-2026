Apply for SSN — week 1
Without it, employer may withhold 24% backup tax. Bring DS-2019, I-94, passport, J1 visa, sponsor letter to the Social Security office.
Unlocks payroll
File W-4 as Non-Resident Alien
Mark single, 0 allowances, write "NRA" on step 4(c). Prevents employer from over-withholding as if you were a US resident.
Prevents overpay
Verify FICA & Medicare not withheld
J1 NRAs are exempt from 7.65%. Check your first paystub. If withheld, flag payroll. If they refuse to refund, file Form 843 + 8316 later.
~$300–500
Ask HR about Form 8233 (US-Ireland treaty)
If you're on J1 Trainee/Intern (not Summer Work Travel), Article 20 can exempt first $20k from federal tax. Confirm category with HR.
~$500–1,500 if eligible
Get sponsor's Sprintax discount code
USIT, SAYIT, CIEE, and InterExchange usually provide free or discounted Sprintax codes — saves ~$50. Ask before you fly.
Saves $50
Open US bank account · set up Wise/Revolut
Chase or BoA, both J1-friendly. Use Wise/Revolut for FX home transfers — saves 2–3% vs Irish bank rates.
Bleed-stopping
eSIM with Mint / US Mobile / Visible
$15–25/month prepaid vs $50–80 with Verizon/AT&T. Set up before flight if possible. Pause Irish plan.
Saves ~$100
Ask manager about Standard staff perks
Staff meal per shift, 25–50% F&B discount, Friends & Family rate at other Standards, TransitChek pre-tax MetroCard.

W-2 arrives end of Jan 2027. Use a trusted US friend's address, NOT your Irish one — international mail loses W-2s constantly.

05Quick reference
Tap a card to expand
Housing — best value neighborhoods
  • Bushwick · Bed-Stuy · Crown Heights — L/M/J/3 trains, target $1,200–1,500
  • Astoria · Ridgewood · Sunnyside — N/W/7, slightly safer feel
  • Washington Heights — A train, cheapest in Manhattan
  • Where to search: Listings Project newsletter (curated), Roomi, SpareRoom, Leasebreak, "J1 NYC Accommodation" FB group
  • Scam red flags: wire transfer, landlord "abroad", no video call, photos that reverse-image to other listings
Drinking without bleeding
  • Happy hour 4–7pm at most spots — half-price drinks, $1 oysters at lots of bars
  • Pre-game with bodega beers in Domino Park, Prospect Park, Brooklyn Bridge Park — saves $40–80/night
  • Dive bars in Bushwick, Bed-Stuy, East Village, LES — $5–7 beers, $10 cocktails
  • Sunday/Monday industry nights — 25–50% off with hospitality ID
  • Never drink as a customer at the venue you'd work at, or its direct neighbours
Free NYC worth doing
  • Staten Island Ferry — free, Statue of Liberty + skyline at sunset
  • MoMA Friday 4–8pm free · Whitney Friday 5–10pm pay-what-you-wish
  • SummerStage — free concerts in Central Park / Prospect Park all summer
  • Shakespeare in the Park — free, TodayTix lottery
  • Bryant Park movie nights Wednesdays · Governors Island ferry free weekday mornings
  • Coney Island · Rockaway Beach — subway ride to the ocean
Side income (off-shift)
  • Paid focus groups — FindFocusGroups.com, Respondent.io · $75–250 per 60–90 min · best $/hour going
  • Catering gigs — Hire Society etc · $20–30/hr cash · weekends
  • Plasma donation — CSL Plasma, BioLife · $50–100 per session, 2x/week
  • Sell before leaving — FB Marketplace 1 week before flight, recoup 40–60% of furniture spend
  • Caveat: J1 secondary work technically needs sponsor approval — most do it but risk is yours
Food cheats — eat well, spend less
  • Trader Joe's is the answer to almost everything — Aldi if there's one near you
  • Halal carts $8–10 huge platters · $1 pizza still exists (99c Fresh, 2 Bros)
  • Bodega egg-and-cheese $4–5 · perfect pre-shift breakfast
  • Dollar dumplings in Chinatown · Smorgasburg Brooklyn weekends
  • Skip Whole Foods, Gristedes, bodega weekly shops, anywhere with table service for lunch
Transit — don't overpay
  • OMNY tap-to-pay on your phone — auto weekly cap at $34 (12 rides Mon–Sun, rest free)
  • Monthly unlimited $132 — only worth it if commuting daily by subway (you probably are)
  • Citi Bike 30-day pass $25 for casual use, watch e-bike fees
  • Walking Manhattan ave-to-ave is faster than the subway for short hops
  • Don't take Uber/Lyft for routine trips — surge pricing will eat a shift's tips
